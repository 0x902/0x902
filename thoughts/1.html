If I were a bird

:: 3/04/2024

If I were a bird, perched nearby,
Watching her with a silent sigh.
Beside her, I'd sit on a tree,
Longing to confess, but too afraid to be.

The day I saw her, in her yellow dress,
Her quiet brown eyes, like a gentle stream.
As friends we stand, in a distant bond,
But deeper feelings, I dare not respond.

For as a bird, I'd chirp and sing,
Hiding the love beneath my wing.
Afraid to soar, afraid to fall,
Lest our friendship crumble, and love's curtain call.

Though love's a whisper in the breeze,
I keep my feelings locked with ease.
In silence, I'll watch from afar,
Content to be just who we are.
